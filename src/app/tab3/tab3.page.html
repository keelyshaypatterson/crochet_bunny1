Videos Slip Knot To make a slip knot make sure the yarn end is infront of your hand. Wrap the yarn around fingers twice makeing two loops. Take the left loop and pull it over the right loop, then pull the right loop over the left loop. Grab the left loop and pull the end yarn and working yarn. Place on hook and pull end yarn until the loop is snug around the loop. Chain Make a slip knot, and insert your hook. Yarn over and pull up a new loop. The first chain stitch is complete. Repeat to make the next chain stitch and the rest of the foundation chain. Single Crochet Insert hook into the next stitch, yarn over, and pull up a loop. Then, yarn over again and pull through both loops on the hook. Half Double Crochet Yarn over, and insert the hook into the next stitch. Yarn over, and pull up a loop. Yarn over again, and pull the yarn through all three loops on the hook. Double Crochet Yarn over. Insert hook into the next stitch, yarn over, and pull up a loop. Yarn over and pull through two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through both loops on the hook. Treble Crochet Yarn over twice. Insert hook into the next stitch, yarn over, and pull up a loop. Yarn over and pull through the first two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the last two loops on the hook. Double Treble Crochet Yarn over three times. Insert hook into the next stitch, yarn over, and pull up a loop. Yarn over and pull through the first two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the last two loops on the hook. Triple Treble Crochet Yarn over four times. Insert hook into the next stitch, yarn over, and pull up a loop. Yarn over and pull through the first two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the next two loops on the hook. Yarn over again and pull through the last two loops on the hook. Magic Ring Take end yarn and wrapping it twice around your two fingers. Making an "X" on the back of your fingers. Insert hook under the "X" yarn over and pull through. Yarn over and pull through the loop making a chain. You can take the magic cirle off your fingers. Insert hook into the middle of the circle, yarn over, and pull back through the circle. Then, yarn over again and pull through both loops on the hook. After you finish your first single crochet stitch continue by working the remaining stitches (the single crochet stitch used in the example) into the circle by repeating steps 5-7, tighten the ring by pulling the yarn end to close the circle. Front Loop Only Each stitch looks like a V with the top of the V pointing in the direction of the hook and the bottom of the V pointing away from the hook. Each side of the V is one of the loops. The loop closest to you is the front. Insert your hook under the front loop and through the middle of the stitch. Then you can continue with whatever stitch you are working with. Back Loop Only Each stitch looks like a V with the top of the V pointing in the direction of the hook and the bottom of the V pointing away from the hook. Each side of the V is one of the loops. The loop farthest to you is the back loop. Insert your hook through the middle and under the back loop. Then you can continue with whatever stitch you are working with.
